
Paracetamol is a common painkiller used to treat aches and pain. It can also be used to reduce a high temperature. It is often used for headaches, muscle pain, backache, and fever.

Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) that reduces inflammation, pain, and fever. It is used for arthritis, menstrual pain, and dental pain. It should be taken with food to reduce stomach upset.

Amoxicillin is a penicillin-type antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections such as chest infections, dental abscesses, and urinary tract infections. It is not effective against viral infections.

ORS (Oral Rehydration Solution) is a mixture of salts and sugars dissolved in water. It is used to prevent or treat dehydration, especially after diarrhea or vomiting. It is a first-line treatment for children and adults with acute diarrhea.

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a condition in which the blood pressure in the arteries is persistently elevated. It can lead to heart disease, stroke, or kidney problems if left untreated. Lifestyle changes and medications like Amlodipine or Losartan are commonly prescribed.

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how the body processes blood sugar (glucose). Type 1 diabetes is insulin-dependent, while Type 2 diabetes is often managed with diet, exercise, and oral medications like Metformin.

Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition where the airways become inflamed and narrowed, making breathing difficult. It is often treated with inhalers such as Salbutamol (reliever) and corticosteroids (preventer).

A sore throat is pain or irritation in the throat that can be caused by viral or bacterial infections. Gargling with salt water, staying hydrated, and using lozenges or throat sprays can help relieve symptoms.

Anemia is a condition in which you lack enough healthy red blood cells to carry adequate oxygen to your body's tissues. Common symptoms include fatigue and weakness. Iron supplements and a diet rich in iron can help treat iron-deficiency anemia.
